I am a Research Scientist at Meta, working on AI/ML systems to enhance how people explore and engage with visual content. I earned my Ph.D. in Computer Science from Columbia University under Brian A. Smith in the Computer-Enabled Abilities Lab, focusing on AI-driven accessibility for blind and low-vision users. My work bridges academia and industry, designing systems that empower exploratory, user-driven interaction with visual information across diverse contexts. I also interned at Apple's Human-Centered Machine Intelligence team, where I worked on AI-driven accessibility solutions.




AI-Driven Interactive Systems for Agency in Access to Visual Experiences

My research focuses on designing interactive systems that leverage AI to empower people with new abilities. A key theme within my work is enabling exploration and agency, particularly for blind and low-vision (BLV) users.

My systems allow BLV users to independently visualize action in sports broadcasts via 3D spatial audio (CHI 2023, UIST 2023), navigate safely using AI-embedded street cameras (ASSETS 2023, UIST 2024), and explore street-level imagery with rich contextual understanding (CHI 2026).

To guide these designs, I conduct qualitative studies to understand user needs. I introduced exploration assistance systems, which enable BLV users to navigate independently rather than relying on turn-by-turn instructions (CSCW 2023), earning an Impact Recognition Award üèÜ.

At Meta, I apply AI/ML to enhance how users engage with video content. My systems enable users to explore long-form videos more effectively--jumping across chapters, surfacing key moments, and engaging with the most relevant information--creating flexible, user-driven ways to understand and enjoy visual content.
